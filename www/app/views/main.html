...Tie your Docker images to a build process and a registry

Edit your Dockerfile

Host your Dockerfile on Github. Simply add one line to your Dockerfile. This is your tie-in point.

Choose a semaphore

Select a way for Bowline to detect your project has been updated (like a Git hook, or polling http, or a custom REST API call)

Let Bowline do the work

Grab a coffee. Bowline will store your image on a local registry, or push to Dockerhub. Of course, after Bowline builds, logs, and alerts you about your build.
